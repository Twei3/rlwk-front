Under the Researcher Links scheme, offered within the Newton Fund, the British Council and the National Natural Science Foundation of China are holding a workshop on the above theme at Chongqing Medical University in Chongqing, China on 15 to 18 October 2018. The workshop is being coordinated by experts from the University College London and Chongqing Medical University. Workshop participants include Early Career Researchers from universities in the UK and in China.

Eligibility: This is an Open Call for participants. Selection of participants will be based on their experience (up to 10 years post-PhD experience), relevance of their research to the workshop, motivation and ability to disseminate the workshop’s outcomes.

The British Council Researcher Links’ programme provides opportunities for early career researchers (ECRs) from the UK and internationally to interact, learn from each other and explore opportunities for building long-lasting research collaborations. During the workshop, early career researchers have the opportunity to present their research in the form of a short oral presentation and discuss it with established researchers and other ECRs. There is a focus on building up links for future collaborations.

Application deadline: Applications close on Thursday 31 May 2018 23:59 GMT.

About the workshop

Respiratory disease is a major cause of mortality and morbidity worldwide, with infants and young children especially susceptible. It is a leading cause of death in infants and children, particularly in middle and low income countries.

The economic consequences of poor respiratory health in childhood, are substantial. Effective techniques for prevention, diagnosis and management of childhood respiratory disease can significantly reduce overall and lung-specific mortality in children and adults.

This workshop will share our understanding of epidemiology, diagnosis and treatment of childhood respiratory diseases in UK and in China. Initial observations will form the basis of future collaborative research studies.

Recommendations from this workshop for the development of novel laboratory and clinical research programmes will lead to highly collaborative, interdisciplinary networks between UK and China.

Draft Workshop Agenda

The 4-day workshop consists of four key themes all related to childhood respiratory disease in UK and China: 1) epidemiology 2) diagnosis and management 3) novel translational methods and 4) new research directions.

The workshop will provide a unique opportunity for sharing research expertise and networking. During the workshops, early career researchers will have the opportunity to present their research in the form of a poster/short oral presentation and discuss this with established researchers from the UK and partner countries.
